Alpha Phi Omega

Alpha Phi Omega is a co-educational national service fraternity. Founded in 1925 in Easton, PA by Frank Reed Horton, the fraternity upholds the beliefs of leadership, friendship and service. Begun as a fraternity associated with the Boy Scouts of America (as it remains today), APO has grown for over 86 years to include 17,000 male and female student leaders on 361 college campuses across the country. Phi Chapter is located in Syracuse, NY and is open to Syracuse University (SU) and SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry (SUNY-ESF) students.


Adopt-A-Street

This is to provide Syracuse University student organizations with a long-term service project that will enhance the quality of life for students, facult and staff living off-campus and aid in the beautification of the off-campuse community. Students can participate in the Adopt-A-Street program at their convenience! You can contact Joe Personte at jpersont@syr.edu if you or your group/club/organization is interested in participating. Adopt-A-Street is a great way to fulfill community service hours! OCCS will provide rubber gloves and trash bags for your use during your experience.

We Rise Above the Streets

We Rise Above the Streets delivers high energy messages that encourage people to live up to their full potential by breaking the cycles of homelessness and crime many face daily. If you are looking for a fulfilling and life changing experience, come help us serve the homeless community in Syracuse. You will be making lunch sacks, hygiene packages and having meaningful exchanges with our homeless friends. Learn about Al-amin's story and powerful impact that one person with compassion can make on an entire community.

OttoTHON:


OttoTHON was founded by Jillian Lynch. Lynch brought the first event to Syracuse University in 2015. Since then, we have grown to raise well over a quarter million dollars for Upstate Golisano Children's Hospital making our mark within this national Dance Marathon movement! All donations from OttoTHON 2019 are going to be used to help build the Golisano Center for Special Needs. This center will provide services to children with disabilities from all across the Upstate New York area. Some of these kids are on waitlists for 2+ years and this center will give them greater access to the resources they need.

Samaritan Center:

It is rooted in the belief that sound nutrition is the first step in enabling people to cope and become productive, self-sufficient members of society. Charity Miles:


Charity Miles is a socially-driven, for-profit company connecting charities, individuals, and corporate sponsors to create social change. The product is an iPhone/Android app enabling you to earn money for charity every time you walk, run, bike, skip, dance or do any human-centered activity. So far our members have earned over $2.5 million to charity! Just choose from 1 of our over 40 charity partners like Feeding America, Pencils of Promise or World Wildlife Fund and get moving. When you finish, you have the option to share your activity on Facebook or Twitter: to build awareness about the cause you supported and to say thanks to your sponsor.
